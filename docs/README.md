## Overview

This README is the entry point to OctoAcme's project management documentation. It summarizes the standard lifecycle and links to detailed process documents used by cross-functional teams. Whether you are a new contributor or a returning team member, start here to understand how OctoAcme plans, executes, and continuously improves its work.

## OctoAcme Project Management Process

OctoAcme uses a structured, iterative lifecycle to move work from idea to measurable outcomes:

1. **Initiation** — Define the problem statement, business goals, success metrics, key stakeholders, and a go/no-go decision for planning.
2. **Planning** — Translate approved work into a prioritized backlog with acceptance criteria, estimates, milestones, dependencies, a Definition of Done, and an initial risk approach.
3. **Execution & Tracking** — Deliver in small increments using team cadences (standups, syncs, demos), follow PR standards, and monitor progress with delivery and quality metrics.
4. **Release & Deployment** — Confirm readiness (acceptance criteria, CI/security checks, release notes, rollback plan), deploy with checklists, and verify post-deploy behavior.
5. **Retrospective & Continuous Improvement** — Capture learnings, assign action items with owners/dates, and feed improvements back into the process docs and backlog.

Supporting practices include clear cross-functional ownership across PM, Product, Developers, QA, and Stakeholders; active risk/dependency management; and consistent communication through weekly status updates, escalation paths, and incident communication templates. Quality assurance spans unit, integration, and end-to-end testing with CI linting and security scanning, and releases require documented readiness checks before deployment.
